# 2008-Global-Financial-Crisis
This project analyzes the 2008 Global Financial Crisis using G7 and India data. It explains causes, impact and recovery highlighting key indicators like GDP ,debt and housing trends

This project analyzes the 2008 Global Financial Crisis using economic data from G7 countries and India.
It explains:
How the recession started
Key causes behind the crisis
Impact on different countries
Lessons learned and prevention strategies
The analysis is based on real economic data from 2000 to 2012

Countries Covered
USA
United Kingdom
Canada
France
Germany

Data & Indicators
The project uses multiple economic indicators, including:
GDP
Inflation
Interest Rates
Unemployment
Housing Price Index
Mortgage Rates
Total Debt

Key Insights
Low interest rates and rising housing prices created a financial bubble
Excessive debt and weak regulations worsened the crisis
The crisis started in the US but quickly spread globally
India remained relatively stable due to strong domestic demand


Root Causes
Subprime mortgage crisis
High leverage and debt
Weak financial regulation
Risky financial products (CDOs, CDS)
Global economic imbalances

Impact
Global GDP declined significantly
Unemployment increased across major economies
Housing markets collapsed
Financial institutions failed or required bailoutsImpact
Global GDP declined significantly

Financial institutions failed or required bailouts


Recovery (2009–2012)
Countries like India and Canada recovered faster
Some countries (like Italy) struggled due to policy decisions
Government interventions played a major role

The 2008 crisis was largely preventable.
Better regulation, responsible lending, and early warning systems can help avoid similar crises in the future.
